Simulating MOSFET: From TCAD to Neural Nets

This project is my implementation of the research paper “SiC MOSFET with Integrated SBD Device Performance Prediction Method Based on Neural Network.” It combines semiconductor device simulation and machine learning to predict key performance metrics of a SiC MOSFET integrated with a Schottky Barrier Diode (SBD). Using Silvaco ATLAS, I modeled the device, extracted I-V characteristics, and automated a parameter sweep for 625 structural variations (P-well doping, depth, JFET width, and SBD width). I also implemented a neural network model to predict critical device parameters such as specific on-resistance (Rₒₙ,sp), threshold voltage (Vₜₕ), and breakdown voltage (BV) from these structural variations. Although hardware limitations prevented running all simulations, the entire pipeline — from device modeling and graph generation to ML-based prediction — is fully implemented and ready for large-scale execution on high-performance systems.

SurveiLens: Video Moment Localization with Natural Language Processing

This is an ongoing research project aimed at enabling natural language-based search within videos, formally known as Video Moment Localization (VML) or Temporal Sentence Grounding (TSG). Unlike most existing work that focuses on short, curated clips, SurveiLens targets long-form videos—such as CCTV footage—where manual search is time-consuming and inefficient.

Muff-Mojo 🧁

"Sprinkle Some Fun on Your Chats🍩"

Muff-Mojo is a browser extension that brings fun and expressive emojis to your typing experience. It suggests and allows easy insertion of emojis as you type words like "smile," "love," or "laugh."
